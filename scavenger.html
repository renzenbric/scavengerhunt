Music is everywhere: radio, TV, internet; people have been downloaded, recorded somebody else music without paying for it for a long time. You may think this is legal. However, copying, downloading, and sharing others work without permission violate copyright law and it is illegal. You can be sued for copyright infringement. This scavenger hunt activity is designed to help you understand the rights of work creators like song writers, compositors, music publishers and the way to use those products without breaking the law.

Basics principles

  • Before downloading a song, make sure the song is in public domain, then no copyright is attached to it.
  • Do not include others song in web site without asking for permission or paying a fee.
  • When you buy a cd song, do not make copies and distribute them to others.
  • Do not post any videos on YouTube with others music.
  • Remember that fair use is a defense not a right.
  • Always assume that a song is copyrighted.
